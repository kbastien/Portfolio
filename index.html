WORKFROM REWARDS

Mobile Design

I organized a 24 hour PDX Product Hunt Hackathon sponsored by Workfrom. During the hackathon, I partnered with the Workfrom team to develop a reward system for their mobile workers. I designed Workfrom Rewards, a platform that rewards individuals when they work from coffee shops, bars, and cafes.

The Model


Workfrom Rewards enables the mobile worker to earn rewards by making purchases at the places they work from around the world. It is a mobile punchcard they can use at any participating location. Workfrom Rewards is a platform that empowers shop owners to effortlessly promote their spaces, incentivize purchases and increase their average revenue per customer. This is for the consumer and the business owner. Creating a meaningful connection between the two — in addition to letting patrons track where they’ve worked from, their rewards status and earn badges and bragging rights along the way.

THE DESIGN


The product experience was influenced by services like Swarm where users check in to the places they are at. We wanted to make a product that did not distract the user from their work, but helped improve it. By providing the user with discounts and pro-tips at the places they work every day, we could improve their remote work. Our goal was to make a frictionless experience between the worker and the shop owners, making it simple and effortless, with as little steps as possible until the user obtains a sense of reward for using the app.

ALASKA SHORE EXCURSIONS

Web Design

A one-stop-shop reservation system that allows you to purchase all of your Alaska Shore Excursions in Juneau, Ketchikan, Skagway, and Sitka at local prices.

Before


Built in 2010, Alaska Shore Excursions was built for utility, where they were focused on providing customers with the best prices on the best tours Alaska has to offer. Being one of the first sites to pop up when you search "alaska shore excursions", the President of the company wanted it to be easy for users to book their Alaska tours, and to provide users with unforgettable experiences in Alaska.

The Redesign


Since Google's mobile-friendly algorithm significantly impacts website rankings in mobile search results, Liquid Alaska wanted to change their website to be mobile responsive. They also wanted to update the design, but keep the same content. I got to work with the team to figure out a solution that contained the same content, but improved the overall user experience. This gave me the opportunity to learn more about the checkout process, aiming to make each user transaction four steps.
